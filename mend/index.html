Overview

/01

What is Mend?

Mend transforms physical therapy into a rewarding experience with personalized goals, guided exercises, and real-time progress tracking from your physical therapist. By integrating gamification elements like streaks, achievements, and milestones, Mend helps users stay motivated throughout their recovery journey.

Over a 9-month span, our team of six used surveys (40 patients, 4 physical therapists) and interviews (6 patients, 3 therapists) to gather insights on motivation and recovery habits. Usability testing at low, mid, and high fidelity refined interactions, while Wizard of Oz testing validated therapist-driven features. Additionally, empathy mapping, journey mapping, flow mapping, and competitor analysis helped identify key pain points and opportunities for improvement.

Problem

/02

Physical therapy offices often overlook the mental aspect of recovery, and patients rarely see progress data, which can make recovery discouraging.

  • 90% of surveyed patients felt unmotivated during their recovery

  • 0% of surveyed patients had access to real progress data while recovering

  • 72% of patients don’t complete at-home exercises, limiting recovery effectiveness

Exploration + Discovery

/03

To understand the motivation gap in physical therapy, we conducted extensive research with both patients and physical therpaists.

  • NUF analysis to identify problems with existing solutions.

  • Empathy mapping to uncover emotional barriers to recovery.

  • Journey mapping to track common user frustrations.

  • Flow mapping to optimize task completion.

  • Competitor analysis to identify gaps in existing recovery tools.

Key interview & survey findings

Physical Therapists

  • Patients need range of motion statistics, strength stats, goal tracking, and personalized recovery programs to stay engaged.

  • A customizable motivational tone (encouraging vs. strict) could improve adherence.

Patients

  • Users wanted mood and pain check-ins, streaks, achievements, and monthly recaps to stay accountable and visualize progress.

  • The lack of progress tracking and tangible results discouraged long-term engagement.

Design Iterations

/04

With a clear understanding of user needs, we iteratively designed Mend’s core experience, refining flows, interactions, and engagement features.

For user flows and wireframing, we defined pages and features we thought might best fit user needs to help inform what our low fidelity prototype would look like and include.

Through prototyping and testing, we conducted low-fidelity usability tests (6 users) to validate task flows, mid-fidelity tests (6 users) to refine UI clarity, and high-fidelity tests (6 users) to ensure real-world usability.

Initial Site Map

Our key flow was defined as the exercise flow, but we also recognized the opprotunity for other beneficial pages such as progress, metrics, and achievements.

Low fidelity testing screens

For low fidelity designs we noticed confusion around the progress page, but tried implementing color to help. Many of our pages felt too blocky as well.

Mid fidelity testing screens

Even implementing color, we still found our designs to be too blocky and gray. The progress page was not resonating with users, so we went for a design overhaul.

High fidelity testing screens

With new colors and layouts, these designs better resonated with users and were significantly easier to use and understand. However, upon testing these pages we still had some upgrades, particularly for goals, achievements, and exercises.

Upon completed high fidelity testing, we had one more round of changes to make.

While we were doing that, the opportunity arose for use to use motion capture in this project, so I suited up into the mocap suit and did the exercises to create 3D models instead of regular videos.

At this point, we realized we needed an icon style to help align our brand further. Custom icons for goals and achievements weren't practical, so we introduced an isometric style to give Mend a stronger visual identity.

Lastly, we introduced dark mode to best account for all user needs and preferences, helping our colors pop more than they do on the light background.

Onboarding Flow

Designed to reduce friction during the user's first-time experience, focusing on progressive disclosure of features and personalization.

Key insights from user research indicated that users enjoyed features like goals and achievements more after better understanding how they worked.

Exercises & Streaks

By completing exercises every day assigned by their physical therapist, users can build a streak.

People were highly satisfied with the ability to see exercises compared to their previous recoverys often relying on pieces of paper to explain how to do exercises.

Goals & Achievements

Some people aren't motivated by just stats, so providing goals and achievements helps motivate people in more tangible and fun ways.

Users enjoyed the personalization of the goals, which are connected to the physical therapists inputs, and the surprise of unlocking achievements, leading to higher user satisfaction around these features

Statistics Tracking

Since very few patients saw their recovery metrics, we thought to dedicate an entire page to these stats. Strenght, range of motion, total numbers, and pain and mood statistsics can all be found here.

The users that cared less about goals and achievements, often were more invested in seeing the numbers around their recovery to stay motivated, helping to further validate this feature.

Check In Flow

To help users track some of their own feelings around recovery, we implemented a check-in flow that tracks pain and mood.

Based on how users respond with their mood and what tone they are set to, they will see a different result on the reflection page.

Recap Flow

To make each step of the recovery feel important, we introduced recaps. This shows the data for their last month in a way that is more celebratory and engaging.

Many user participants stated that this reminds them of Spotify Wrapped, which was exactly what we were going for.
