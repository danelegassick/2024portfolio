Hermae

Design system

[Onboarding flow]

When onboarding people into a new product, how can we best streamline the experience when there are significant loading times and deliver an "Aha" moment?

Overview

/01

Hermae is a business focused on optimizing design systems and providing teams with an AI to help streamline component use and onboarding developers.

By uploading a Storybook file (a workshop environment for UI components), the AI would build a chat system specific to your design system working as an assistant. I was brought on to design an onboarding process for the emerging business.

This was a freelance opportunity taken on by Benjamin Moon and myself over the course of 2 months with the goal of building our experience in different UX work environments.

Problem

/02

Onboarding should engage users with the value of the product and how it works. How might we show what makes Hermae worth using?

When onboarding for a product like Hermae, long loading times and confusion might cause user dropoff. So we found that, providing support and guidance through the process can help keep people around.

To best understand how other complex services handle onboarding, we did some competitor analysis.

Research

/03

Competitor Analysis

Notion

Through pre-populated templates, users can see immediate value in the service and get started quickly.

Slack

Focuses on guided experiences to avoid complexity. Users are introduced to and encouraged to try features one at a time to avoid overwhelming users.

Duolingo

Emphasizes interactive learning by providing a guided experience that takes users through some key features and highlights the value of Duolingo.

We understood that the best way to engage users was to show them what the chatbot could do. This would create an interactiv learning experience and an "Wow-factor" moment that could show the value.

This still left us with one problem: loading times. Uploading a storybook file can take a while, and building an AI model around that file is no quick task. How could we keep people engaged throughout that process?

To best optimize time, upon opening the onboarding flow, users would upload storybook first, then go through company details to allow the AI to load the information in the process. To keep engagement after than, we thought to use visuals from their storybook file to make the loading feel more tangible.

Designs

/04

The onboaring flow can be separated into two key sections. Input/loading & the value showcase.

The input/loading flow is where users will upload their info and develop their account while the product is loading.

The value showcase is designed to make a personal experience for the user showing the ways in which they can prompt the chatbot for information, showing personalized results based on the Storybook file they uploaded.

Input/Loading Flow

By being transparent upfront about the process, we hope to help people expect longer loading times. We even encourage them to grab a coffee once they add their company info.

3JS Loading AI Information

As information was uploaded into the AI system, it would be populated in a 3JS environment to create what feels like a solar system. This was chosen to mirror design system language with an engaging visual.

The value showcased was designed to show a simulated back and forth with the chatbot using personalized data from the Storybook upload.
